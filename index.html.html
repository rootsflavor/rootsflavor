04 — Your Biggest Opportunity

Rasilo Agriculture — You Are Underusing Them

Rasilo buys 65+ products. You supply 2. They are already your client — the trust barrier is lower than with any new buyer. The fastest way to grow your revenue is not to find new clients, it is to supply more products to Rasilo.

Action this week: Visit Rasilo in person — not WhatsApp. Ask them directly: "Which of your 65 products are you struggling to source consistently or at good prices?" Then see if any of those come from hill regions you can access.

How To Build The Relationship

Right now it is purely transactional — they see you as a vendor. One face-to-face conversation where you show genuine interest in their business problems changes this. Bring a small sample of something new from Dolpa. Show them you are building something serious.

A client who trusts you personally is 10x harder to replace than one who just compares prices. This is your most important relationship in the business right now.

Negotiating A Better Price

Once you have 2–3 other buyers, come back to Rasilo and renegotiate. Right now they control the price because you need them. When you have options, you have leverage. Do not renegotiate until you have those other buyers lined up — but plan to do it within 6 months.

08 — Risk Management

What Could Go Wrong & How To Handle It

High
Cousin leaves before harvest

Your entire supply chain collapses. Fix: Have the rate conversation this week. Also build 3–4 direct farmer contacts during your Dolpa visit so you are not 100% dependent on one person.

High
Only Rasilo buys — stock takes 8+ months to sell

Loan repayments start before you recover your investment. Fix: Get 2–3 buyers committed before spending loan money on stock. Non-negotiable.

High
Truck unavailable or too expensive during harvest season

Everyone wants trucks during harvest. You may compete for availability. Fix: Book and deposit with a transporter during your Dolpa visit — months before harvest.

Medium
Stock damaged in storage (mold, pests)

Grains stored wrongly in Kathmandu humidity = total loss on that stock. Fix: HDPE bags, proper ventilation, pest control before first delivery arrives. Spend NPR 25–30K on this — it protects lakhs of stock.

Medium
Cash flow gap — paying farmers before receiving buyer payment

You pay upfront in Dolpa, but Rasilo may pay 30–60 days later. Fix: Negotiate 30–50% advance payment from buyers on large orders. Keep emergency reserve untouched for this gap.

Low
Rasilo reduces orders or switches supplier

Purely transactional relationship means no loyalty. Fix: Build personal relationship now. Have backup buyers lined up before harvest so you are never dependent on one client.

But the business lives or dies on two things in the next 10 weeks: keeping your cousin and finding more buyers. Everything else — the loan, the truck, the storage — follows from those two things being sorted.

If you secure 3–4 buyers and fill that truck with Chino-heavy stock, a realistic Year 1 target is NPR 6–8L turnover with NPR 1.5–2L net profit — after loan repayment. That is a real, sustainable business. And it is within reach.
